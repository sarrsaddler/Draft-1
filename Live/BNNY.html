IBM x BNNY

A case study of how I helped an internal business resource group get a few steps closer to align their visual identity through fundamental branding concepts.

The information present below are my own views and do not represent IBM.

ABOUT

The Black Network of New York (BNNY) is the Tri-state area Business Resource Group serving IBM's black professionals. BNNY exists to bring together black professionals within IBM to network, mentor and support each other as a community so that we each can achieve collectively and individually.

ROLE

Art Director

Responsibilities
  • Work alongside marketing chair to determine visual communication on channels such as email, slack (ibmbnny.slack.com), The Drum connections community, update BNNY's website.
  • Review and approve the designs of other board members
  • Iterating and managing the Black Network of New York brand identity.
Outcomes:
  • * The BNNY Logo
  • * Color Palette

COLOR PALETTE

My first step in choosing the color palette was identifying what the colors had to represent. The colors had to represent the black network of New York City being apart of a major corporation based in a city filled with immigrants. I also had to understand that the Black experience is different for each member of the group. The colors I used for the group was my attempt to capture the vibrancy of black culture and to remain within the IBM brand. My main source of inspiration came from looking at Caribbean flags, Kwanzaa, and different music genre like Reggae.

Logo process from rough sketches(left) to vector mockup(right).

THE BNNY LOGO

The chair of the BNNY board asked for my help on creating a new logo. Immediately I began to think about what this logo should represent because I believe a logo should be functional and able to communicate the organization. In my early attempts of logo design, I wanted to convince the board to move away from the name black network of new york and choose a different name like Melanin. I noticed that Black network of New York ended with three Ks. The three K's was a problem for me because I thought of the Ku Klux Klan(KKK). The KKK through America history has been a white supremacist organization that oppressed people of color. If the logo was supposed to be effective I thought it should empower people of color to organized.

Due to company restrictions, we couldn't change our name. However, I did come up with a design that stresses the importance of people of color by putting Black in bold characters and on top of everything else.
